EntitlementIQ — Identity Risk Dashboard

A working prototype dashboard for scoring and triaging identity risk — built to explore how access governance teams could prioritize reviews when every account can't get equal attention.

Stack
HTML / JS, client-side prototype
Role
Product design & prototype build
Status
Interactive prototype

The problem

Access governance teams typically review entitlements on a fixed schedule — quarterly access certifications, periodic audits — regardless of whether an account's risk has changed. A service account that's been dormant for months and a user account with a recent spike in unusual access requests get the same review cadence, even though they represent very different levels of risk.

The prototype explores a simple question: if every identity had a live risk rating, what would the workday of someone managing access reviews actually look like — and how would a risk score change what they do next?

The prototype

EntitlementIQ is a four-screen workflow: a dashboard surfacing risk-rated accounts (HIGH / LOW badges), a request-access flow, an access-reviews queue for approvers, a "my access" view for an individual user, and a risk-scoring API tester that shows how a rating is composed from underlying signals.

Product thinking behind it

The interesting design problem isn't the risk score itself — it's where the score shows up and what it changes about a reviewer's behavior. A HIGH risk badge on a dormant service account should change the review queue's ordering, not just add a color to a table. The prototype keeps the risk signal visible at every step — dashboard, review queue, and individual access view — so risk isn't something you check separately from the workflow, it's part of the workflow.

This connects directly to the NHI/NUA governance work in the experience timeline: non-human identities (service accounts, API tokens, machine credentials) are exactly the population where risk signals matter most and get the least continuous attention, because nobody's "using" them day to day in a way that surfaces anomalies the way a human's login pattern would.

Outcome

5 views
dashboard, request access, access reviews, my access, and a risk API tester in one workflow
2 tiers
HIGH / LOW risk badges drive what a reviewer sees first
1 question
does the risk signal change behavior, or just decorate a table?
